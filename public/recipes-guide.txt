RECIPE 1: NIMAR STYLE BANANA CHIPS CHAAT
--------------------------------------------------------------------
A quick, tangy, and crunchy street-style chaat using fresh banana chips.

Ingredients:
- 100g Zerothi Salted or Tomato Banana Chips
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 1 small tomato, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder
- A pinch of red chilli powder

Instructions:
1. Spread the Zerothi Banana Chips on a wide serving plate.
2. Evenly sprinkle chopped onions, tomatoes, and fresh coriander over the chips.
3. Sprinkle the cumin powder and red chilli powder.
4. Drizzle fresh lemon juice right before serving.
5. Toss lightly and eat immediately to enjoy the ultimate crunch!

RECIPE 2: TRADITIONAL BILONA GHEE SHAKARKAND HALWA
--------------------------------------------------------------------
A rich, fragrant sweet potato pudding cooked in pure wood-churned cow ghee.

Ingredients:
- 3 medium sweet potatoes (shakarkand), boiled and mashed
- 3 tbsp Zerothi Pure Cow Ghee (Bilona Churned)
- 1/2 cup organic jaggery powder
- 1/4 tsp green cardamom powder
- 1 tbsp chopped almonds and cashews

Instructions:
1. Heat Zerothi Pure Cow Ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan on medium heat.
2. Add the mashed sweet potatoes and sauté for 8-10 minutes until they turn golden-brown and release a rich, nutty ghee aroma.
3. Add the jaggery powder and mix well. Sauté for another 5 minutes until the jaggery melts and blends completely.
4. Stir in the cardamom powder and chopped nuts.
5. Serve warm. The granular texture of Zerothi ghee elevates this simple rustic sweet.

RECIPE 3: NIMAR WOOD-PRESSED GROUNDNUT OIL POORI
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Crispy, puffy whole wheat flatbreads deep-fried in chemical-free groundnut oil.

Ingredients:
- 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
- 1 tbsp Zerothi Pure Cow Ghee (melted)
- 1/2 tsp salt
- Water (as needed to knead a firm dough)
- Zerothi Wood-Pressed Groundnut Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions:
1. Combine wheat flour, salt, and melted ghee in a mixing bowl. Mix well.
2. Add water gradually and knead into a firm, tight dough. Rest the dough for 15 minutes.
3. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll them out into circles of even thickness.
4. Heat Zerothi Wood-Pressed Groundnut Oil in a deep kadhai until hot.
5. Gently slide a rolled poori into the hot oil. Press lightly with a slotted spoon until it puffs up.
6. Flip and fry until golden brown on both sides. Drain on a paper towel. Serve hot with potato curry!

RECIPE 4: NIMAR SPICY BANANA CHIPS SALAD
--------------------------------------------------------------------
A protein-rich sprouted salad topped with spicy banana chips.

Ingredients:
- 1 cup mixed sprouts (mung, chana), boiled
- 50g Zerothi Peri-Peri or Pudina Banana Chips (crushed)
- 1/2 cucumber, finely chopped
- 1/2 pomegranate seeds
- 1 tbsp mint-coriander chutney
- Chaat masala to taste

Instructions:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine boiled sprouts, chopped cucumber, and pomegranate seeds.
2. Toss with mint-coriander chutney and a pinch of chaat masala.
3. Right before serving, gently crush Zerothi Peri-Peri Banana Chips over the salad.
4. Mix gently and serve. The fiery peri-peri crunch provides the perfect contrast to the fresh sprouts.

RECIPE 5: GHEE-ROASTED NIMAR SPICED MAKHANA
--------------------------------------------------------------------
A healthy, crunchy regional snack perfect for tea-time.

Ingredients:
- 2 cups lotus seeds (makhana)
- 2 tbsp Zerothi Pure Cow Ghee
- 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
- 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
- 1/2 tsp black salt

Instructions:
1. Heat Zerothi Pure Cow Ghee in a pan on low heat.
2. Add the makhanas and roast on low-medium flame for 10-12 minutes, stirring continuously, until they become crunchy.
3. Turn off the flame. Immediately add turmeric powder, black pepper powder, and black salt.
4. Toss well so that the residual heat coats the spices evenly onto the makhanas with the ghee.
5. Let cool completely and store in an airtight container.
